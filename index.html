Mercury

Mercury, the smallest planet in our solar system, is closest to the Sun. It has a rocky, cratered surface and undergoes extreme temperature changes. Despite being near the Sun, Mercury lacks an atmosphere to trap heat, resulting in drastic temperature variations. Its surface is marked by numerous craters, evidence of its tumultuous history. The planet's proximity to the Sun means it completes an orbit in just 88 Earth days, making its year the shortest of all the planets. Mercury's lack of atmosphere and its rapid orbit make it a unique and fascinating object of study in our solar system.
